On Wednesday, a lawyer for Musk sent a letter to Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg, accusing his company of systematically stealing Twitter’s trade secret to build Threads, Semafor reports(Opens in a new window).   Elon Musk's lawyer is accusing Meta of stealing trade secrets from Twitter to build Threads by hiring dozens of former Twitter employees.   “Twitter intends to strictly enforce its intellectual property rights, and demands that Meta take immediate steps to stop using any Twitter trade secrets or other highly confidential information,” the letter adds. The letter(Opens in a new window) claims Meta hired “dozens of former Twitter employees” who had access to the company’s trade secrets and other confidential information. “With that knowledge, Meta deliberately assigned these employees to develop, in a matter of months, Meta’s copycat ‘Threads’ app,” the letter says.
