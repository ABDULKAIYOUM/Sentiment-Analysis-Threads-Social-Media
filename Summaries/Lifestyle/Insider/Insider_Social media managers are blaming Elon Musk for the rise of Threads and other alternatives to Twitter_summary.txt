"Lighting a candle for all the social media managers who are panic-writing the team (Thread) strategy tonight," one Twitter user wrote about Meta's new platform.   Threads — which Twitter cofounder Jack Dorsey dubbed one of "seven Twitter clones" — opened for business on Wednesday night and now has more than 80 million users, per one tracker.   With the launch of Meta's Threads and Black Twitter's migration to the Spill app, social media managers say they're overwhelmed by all the new platforms. For social media managers, this means developing a presence on new apps outside of Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. New social-networking apps rivaling Twitter seem to be emerging almost every day, and it's taking its toll on workers in the industry.
